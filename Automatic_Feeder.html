Automatic Plant Feeder / Fertilizer

Final Design

This project was to design a solution to an everyday problem, that we as students face. Along with a team of 3 other students, we developed an automatic plant feeder and fertilizer dispenser. We researched the problem, current solutions, and developed a design that not only allowed the user to set the amount of water but also the amount of liquid fertilizer to be dispensed for a wide range of plants.

ProtoTyping

During the prototyping phase of this project, I learned the importance of GD&T, tolerance stackup, and how to use them to ensure that parts fit together correctly. Additionally, I learnt the importance of designing for manufacturability, and how to optimize parts for CNC machining and watercutting. As well as many different CAD good practices, such as simple sketches and creating drawings for readability.

Manufacturing

Throughout this project I gained proficiency in manufacturing processes and applied them to component design. We evaluated and selected processes optimized for high-volume production and identified materials that provided performance for the intended application on the final design. Additionally, for the prototype design, we optimized parts manufacturability for CNC machining and watercutting, as well as picking materials that were easy to work with and readily available.

CVT Dyno

Design

Our car uses a custom CVT, which requires extensive testing and validation to reach optimal performance. In order to accurately mimic the effects of the torque loads the CVT sees we have created a dyno. As a freshman year project, I was tasked with designing a heat shroud for the dyno, which is used to protect the user from the heat generated and to direct the heat to a cooling fan.

Analysis

Additionaly, as part of my work on the dyno I designed and validated an adaptor between the gearbox and the motor using FEA to ensure safe operation. The adaptor was designed to be a trilobe shape to match the output of the gearbox, and was validated using Ansys to ensure it could withstand the torque loads it would see during operation.

Manufacturing

In order to manufacture the heat shroud, I use a waterjet to cut the steel sheet to the correct dimensions, bent it, and welded it together. The adaptor was manufactured by using a manual lathe to turn the part to the correct dimensions, and then CNC'ing the trilobe shape and offset holes into the part.
